Zero Robotics and the Space Systems Laboratory

Summer '17

technical skills: front end and back end development, data management
soft skills: working on a team, public speaking

In the Summer of 2017 I worked with the Space Systems Laboratory to develop the High School version of the Zero Robotics competition for that year. This project involved working with both the back-end (written in C++) and the front-end (written in TypeScript) sides of the game. The 2017 competitionâ€™s playing field was a grid each with a randomly determined height. My main role involved storing the height for each of the columns efficiently by storing two heights in a single integer then decoding it on the front-end side and displaying the columns ensuring that the heights corresponded correctly.

This taught me how to work with a team of developers to learn the previous team of developers code and generate new code to get closer to the final product. Because the final competition runs on small spheres on the ISS, the biggest constraint we faced was that of memory. Learning how to manage our data effectively and still have a working product was a great experience.
