SeeBoat

fall '17

technical skills: arduino, featherboard, benchtop oscilliscope work
soft skills: reporting to a supervisor, system integration

In the fall of 2017, I did research with the Object Based Media Group inside the Media Lab working on a project called SeeBoat. SeeBoat is a fleet of remote controlled boats that measure and display water quality in real time. Each boat is equipped with temperature, turbidity, conductivity, and pH sensors and LEDs that change color based on real time data.

I had three tasks. First, I debugged and calibrated the conductivity sensor. This sent a square wave generated by our arduino-based board into the water and through some op-amp based signal processing and determined a conductivity value for our environment.Second, I set up the pH sensor to send us measurements every second and communicate well with the GPS which was also on the I2C bus. Finally, I compiled all the previous software into a functional system that could send our data via radio waves to another board.

SeeBoat threw me headfirst into the world of EE. Over the summer I switched from Computer Science to Electrical Engineering and knew very little about the field. Immediately I was soldering op-amps and working with RC filters and signal processing and I had to learn how to work fast and effectively.
